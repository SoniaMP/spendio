## Overview

### Problem
People who track personal spending in spreadsheets or generic finance apps lose visibility on three things at once: where the money goes by category, how this month compares to the previous one, and who owes them back for shared purchases. Existing tools either focus on couples/teams (Splitwise) or on bank aggregation (open banking apps), but not on "my personal expenses, with optional partial sharing".

### Value proposition

- For **the owner user**, a frictionless way to log expenses and see — at a glance — how much, on what category, and on which sheet they spent this month vs. the previous one.
- For **invited users**, read or edit access to specific sheets without exposing the rest of the owner's finances.

### Differentiator

- Personal-first model: sheets and categories are owned by one user; sharing is opt-in per sheet.
- Clean separation between the original expense (reconcilable with the bank) and partial reimbursements from third parties (effective cost). No debt-tracking ledger.
- No bank connection, no investments, no budgets — narrow scope on purpose.

### Positioning
A personal expense tracker with optional sheet sharing, not a team-accounting or debt-settlement tool.

## User roles

### Owner user
The account holder. Creates sheets, categories and expenses. Authenticates via email + password or Google OAuth. Owns and can share their own sheets.

### Invited user
Another user granted access to a shared sheet, with `read` or `edit` permission. If the invited email does not yet have an account, a *stub* user is created and activated through the standard password-reset flow.

### Anti-personas (NOT target users)
- Couples/teams that need full split-bill or settle-up workflows (use Splitwise).
- Users that want bank aggregation, budgets or investment tracking (use Fintonic / YNAB / similar).

## Macro features

- **[Authentication](features/00.authentication.md)** — Google OAuth, email + password, password reset, stub-account activation.
- **[Expenses](features/01.expenses.md)** — CRUD of expenses, per-user categories, monthly filtering, monthly total + MoM comparison, category breakdown, pie/bar charts, Excel export. In design: shared payments. Planned: recurring expenses with alerts, forecast.

## Global rules

### Data & privacy
- User data is private by default. Sharing is explicit and per-sheet.
- Payments attached to an expense (when the feature ships) are private to the expense creator, even on shared sheets.
- Emails are stored and matched case-insensitively.

### Performance
- Monthly listings (expenses + aggregates) come pre-aggregated from the backend. No N+1 when rendering tables or charts.

### Auth & security
- The system never reveals whether an email exists (no account enumeration).
- Password-reset links expire after 30 minutes and are invalidated after a single use.
- Password-reset requests are rate-limited to 5 per hour per email.

### i18n & accessibility
- Spanish is the primary language. UI strings live in centralized resource files.
- Components are built on Radix primitives (accessible base), not formally audited.

## Out of scope

### Not included
- **Open banking / bank aggregation**: out of product mission; we want manual, intentional logging.
- **Budgets and monthly spending alerts**: tracking ≠ budgeting; would change the product's personality.
- **Multi-currency**: complexity (FX rates, historical conversion) not justified by demand.
- **General income tracking** (paychecks, standalone transfers): Spendio is an expense app; income leaks would muddy the model.
- **Investments and net worth**: separate domain.
- **2FA**: not enough usage to justify the UX cost yet.
- **Receipt OCR**: nice-to-have, large engineering cost.
